**## 📊 OVERVIEW**



I analyzed sales data to identify revenue trends, seasonal patterns, customer behavior, and business risks. The analysis shows strong year-over-year growth (\~30–35%) with clear seasonal spikes in November. While the business demonstrates healthy growth and strong customer retention, deeper analysis reveals moderate revenue concentration, delayed customer retention patterns, and opportunities to improve customer lifetime value.



\---



**## 📈 DETAILED ANALYSIS**



**### 🔹 Revenue \& Growth Trends**



\* Revenue grew approximately 30–35% from 2003 to 2004, indicating strong business expansion.

\* The apparent decline in 2005 is due to partial-year data rather than an actual drop in performance.

\* Month-over-month growth shows high volatility (-74% to +115%), reflecting seasonal demand fluctuations.



\---



**### 🔹 Seasonal Trends**



\* Sales consistently peak in November (\~$1M+), with October acting as a strong lead-in month.

\* This confirms a clear year-end seasonal demand pattern.

\* Rolling 3-month average analysis highlights underlying trends by smoothing short-term fluctuations.

\* The sharp spike in November followed by normalization indicates seasonal surges rather than sustained growth.



\---



**### 🔹 Customer Contribution (Pareto Insight)**



\* Top 5 customers contribute approximately 20–21% of total revenue, indicating moderate concentration.

\* Pareto analysis shows that \~60%+ of customers are required to reach 80% of total revenue.

\* This indicates a relatively diversified customer base with lower dependency risk compared to typical 80/20 distribution.



\---



**### 🔹 Product Performance**



\* The leading product line contributes nearly 40% of total revenue.

\* This highlights strong product dominance but also introduces concentration risk.



\---



**### 🔹 Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)**



\* High-value customers generate revenue primarily through frequent repeat purchases rather than single large transactions.

\* Average order value remains consistent (\~3000–4000), indicating stable purchasing behavior.

\* Some customers exhibit high order value but low purchase frequency, representing an opportunity to increase engagement.

\* A large portion of customers have low purchase frequency (2–3 orders), indicating potential to improve customer lifetime value.



\---



**### 🔹 Customer Retention (Cohort Analysis)**



\* Immediate retention (next-month return) is low, with most customers not returning quickly after their first purchase.

\* Delayed retention is observed, with customers returning after 2–4 months.

\* Larger cohorts tend to show relatively better retention behavior.

\* Retention declines over time, indicating customer churn and opportunities for engagement strategies.



\---



**### 🔹 Risk \& Opportunity Insights**



\* Revenue concentration across top customers and product lines introduces business risk.

\* Seasonal dependency creates revenue spikes but also volatility.

\* Opportunities exist to:



&#x20; \* Improve customer retention

&#x20; \* Increase repeat purchases

&#x20; \* Convert low-frequency customers into high-value customers

&#x20; \* Diversify product and customer base



\---



**## 🚀 CONCLUSION**



The business demonstrates strong growth, clear seasonal demand patterns, and healthy customer retention. However, moderate revenue concentration, delayed retention behavior, and reliance on seasonal spikes highlight the need for strategic improvements in customer engagement, diversification, and demand planning to ensure long-term stability.
