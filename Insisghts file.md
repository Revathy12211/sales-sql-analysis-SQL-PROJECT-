**OVERVIEW**

I analyzed sales data to identify growth trends, seasonal patterns, and customer behavior. I found that revenue grew about 30% YoY, with strong peaks in November driven by seasonal demand. I also identified that the top 5 customers contribute around 20% of revenue and the top product line contributes about 40%, indicating concentration risk. Additionally, I analyzed month-over-month growth which showed high volatility, and evaluated customer retention where about 68% of customers are repeat buyers. Overall, the analysis highlighted both growth opportunities and potential risks in revenue dependency.



**DETAILED ANALYSIS**



Revenue grew \~30–35% from 2003 to 2004, indicating strong expansion. Although revenue appears to decline in 2005, this is due to partial-year data rather than actual business contraction 



Sales consistently peak in November for both 2003 (\~$1.03M) and 2004 (\~$1.09M), with October acting as a strong lead-in month (\~$550K+), confirming a clear year-end seasonal demand pattern.

Each year shows a distinct peak-performing month contributing disproportionately higher revenue, suggesting opportunities for targeted promotional strategies



Top 5 customers contribute approximately 15–20% of total revenue, indicating high dependency on a small customer segment and potential revenue risk.



The leading product line contributes nearly 40% of total sales, demonstrating clear product dominance and opportunity for focused investment.



Month-over-month growth shows extreme volatility ranging from -74.57% to +115.28%, indicating highly seasonal demand patterns and potential one-time sales spikes.



While 98.9% of customers have placed more than one order, a more meaningful retention metric shows \~68.5% of customers placing multiple repeat orders (>2), indicating strong customer loyalty and sustained engagement.



Revenue concentration across limited customers and product lines introduces business risk, where loss of key contributors could significantly impact total sales.
