📊 Sales Performance Analysis using SQL

🔍 Problem Statement
The business wants to understand revenue trends, customer behavior, and product performance to identify growth opportunities and risks.

🎯 Objective
To analyze sales data and answer key business questions:

How is revenue growing over time?
Are there seasonal patterns in sales?
Is revenue dependent on a few customers or products?
How strong is customer retention?

🧠 Approach
Used SQL (CTE, Window Functions, Aggregations,Case when)
Performed time-based analysis (YoY, MoM)
Evaluated customer and product contribution
Identified trends and anomalies in sales

🛠 Tools & Technologies
- SQL Server
- SSMS (SQL Server Management Studio)

📂 Dataset Description
The dataset contains sales transactions with the following key fields:
- `ORDERNUMBER` – Unique order ID
- `ORDERDATE` – Date of order
- `SALES` – Revenue generated
- `CUSTOMERNAME` – Customer name
- `PRODUCTLINE` – Product category
- `YEAR_ID`, `MONTH_ID` – Time dimensions

📈 Key Insights
Revenue grew by ~30–35% YoY, indicating strong business growth
Sales peak in November, showing clear seasonal demand
Top 5 customers contribute ~20% revenue → dependency risk
One product line contributes ~40% → product concentration
MoM growth is highly volatile (-74% to +115%)
~68% customers are repeat buyers → strong retention

⚠️ Business Impact
Business is growing but depends on few customers/products
Seasonal spikes can be leveraged for marketing
Risk mitigation needed through diversification

💡 Recommendations
Expand customer base to reduce dependency
Promote underperforming product lines
Use seasonal trends for targeted campaigns
Improve demand planning for volatility# 📊 Sales Data Analysis using SQL
