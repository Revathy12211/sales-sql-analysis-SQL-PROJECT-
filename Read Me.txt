# 📊 Sales Data Analysis using SQL

---

## 🧾 Overview
This project analyzes a sales dataset using SQL to uncover key business insights related to revenue trends, customer behavior, product performance, and growth patterns.

The analysis focuses on identifying seasonality, revenue concentration, customer retention, and potential business risks.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze year-over-year (YoY) and month-over-month (MoM) growth
- Identify peak sales periods and seasonal trends
- Evaluate customer contribution and retention
- Analyze product line performance
- Assess revenue concentration and business risk

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- SQL Server
- SQL (CTE, Window Functions, Aggregations)
- SSMS (SQL Server Management Studio)

---

## 📂 Dataset Description
The dataset contains sales transactions with the following key fields:
- `ORDERNUMBER` – Unique order ID
- `ORDERDATE` – Date of order
- `SALES` – Revenue generated
- `CUSTOMERNAME` – Customer name
- `PRODUCTLINE` – Product category
- `YEAR_ID`, `MONTH_ID` – Time dimensions

---

## 📊 Key Analysis Performed

### 🔹 1. Sales Trend Analysis
- Total revenue calculation
- Year-wise and month-wise sales trends

### 🔹 2. Growth Analysis
- Year-over-Year (YoY) growth using `LAG`
- Month-over-Month (MoM) growth analysis

### 🔹 3. Customer Analysis
- Top 5 customers by revenue
- Repeat customer behavior analysis

### 🔹 4. Product Analysis
- Product line contribution to total revenue
- Identification of top-performing product categories

### 🔹 5. Time-Based Insights
- Best month per year
- Seasonal sales pattern detection

---

## 🔥 Key Insights

- Revenue grew by approximately **30–35% from 2003 to 2004**, indicating strong business expansion  
- Sales peak consistently in **November (~$1M+)**, confirming strong year-end seasonal demand  
- Revenue is heavily concentrated in **October–November**, highlighting seasonal dependency  
- Top 5 customers contribute **~15–20% of total revenue**, indicating moderate customer concentration risk  
- Leading product line contributes **~40% of total sales**, showing strong product dominance  
- MoM growth ranges from **-74.57% to +115.28%**, indicating high sales volatility  
- Approximately **68.5% of customers are repeat buyers (>2 orders)**, showing strong customer retention  
- Revenue concentration across key customers and products introduces **potential business risk**

---

## ⚠️ Business Recommendations

- Diversify customer base to reduce dependency on top customers  
- Expand product portfolio to reduce reliance on dominant product lines  
- Leverage seasonal peaks (Nov–Dec) for targeted marketing campaigns  
- Implement loyalty programs to maximize repeat customer value  
- Improve demand planning to handle high sales volatility

## 🚀 Conclusion
The analysis highlights strong revenue growth and clear seasonal trends, along with high customer retention. However, revenue concentration and volatility suggest the need for strategic planning to ensure long-term business stability.
